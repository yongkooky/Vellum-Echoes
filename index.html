The Jade Compass of the Wulin Forest

Deep in the forest, the compass needle did not point north. It pointed down.

Lei Fang had been a mapmaker's apprentice for three years before she found the compass in her master's locked chest — a disc of pale green jade with no markings, no mechanism, and yet it moved. The forest of Wulin was three days east. She left before dawn, before she could change her mind.

A Thief in the Hall of Ten Thousand Years

She had memorised the palace in the dark. She had not counted on the emperor being awake.

The imperial palace of Changan slept at the third hour — all except the Emperor's study, where a light had burned every night for thirty years. Xiu Mei knew every corridor, every guard rotation, every loose floor tile. What she did not know was why the Emperor never slept, and what he kept reading in that warm, solitary light.
